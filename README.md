# CS330

1. How do I approach designing software?
I approach designing software by starting with the overall goal and what I want the final result to look like. Then I break it down into smaller pieces and build it step by step. In this project, I started with a basic layout of my scene and added objects first, then went back and refined details like size, placement, and lighting. One new skill I developed was thinking in 3D and understanding how lighting, textures, and positioning all work together. This process of starting simple and improving over time is something I can apply to future projects to stay organized and avoid getting overwhelmed.

2. How do I approach developing programs?
When developing programs, I like to take things one step at a time and test as I go. For this project, I used a lot of trial and error, especially with lighting and object placement. Iteration was a big part of my process because I constantly had to adjust values, rerun the program, and make small changes until everything looked right. Over time, my approach evolved from just trying to get things working to focusing more on how everything looked and how the code worked together. I also became more comfortable troubleshooting and fixing issues as they came up.

3. How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?
Computer science helps me build problem solving skills and think more logically, which is important for my goal of going into data analytics. This project gave me a better understanding of how visual systems work and helped me develop technical and critical thinking skills that will be useful in my future. Professionally, even if I’m not working directly with graphics, these skills still apply. Being able to visualize information, understand systems, and troubleshoot problems will help me in any role where I need to analyze data and present it in a clear way.
